Analysis of Coupling and Cohesion


In computer science, coupling and cohesion go hand in hand. Coupling is defined as the degree of interaction between software modules while cohesion is the degree of interaction from within a software module. A good program design uses a low degree of coupling and a high degree of cohesion. Our design has a low degree of coupling. The only interactions between separate modules happens when information is being gathered for sending reports or when databases are being accessed or updated. Our design also has a high degree of cohesion. The methods contained within classes are related only to the class in which it is contained. Most of the methods are setters and getters which are perfect examples of methods which have a specific purpose related to the module in which it is being contained and does not affect other modules or require info from other modules.

  8. The provider totals the fees for that week at the end of the week.

Generate Reports Use Case

Activity Diagram

Context

This activity diagram gives an overview of sending reports to members, providers, and ChocAn managers. Member reports are email attachments sent to members at midnight on Fridays (or when a manager requests). Provider reports are email attachments sent to each provider who has billed ChocAn during the week. Summary reports include a list of every provider to be paid for the week, the number of consultations each, and their total fee.

Actors

Member, Manager, ChocAn Data Center, Provider

Main Success Scenario

  1. On Fridays, a process in the ChocAn Data Center begins that does the accounting for the week.
  2. The system compiles the reports.
  3. The system sens the member, provider, and summary reports to their respective party.


Exceptions

At any point during the week, the manager can request this report and have the accounting process completed and receive the report as an email attachment.

Glossary

Chocoholics Anonymous (ChocAn)

An organization dedicated to helping people who are addicted to chocolate.

ChocAn Members

Individuals who pay the monthly fee to ChocAn and participate in the various programs that the organization offers.

Member Card

A plastic card with the member's name, nine digit member number, and a magnetic strip containing that information.

Provider

A Health Care Professional that provides services to ChocAn members.

Provider Number

Identification number to distinguish health care professionals.

ChocAn Computer Terminal

Similar to a credit card reader in a shop, the terminal prompts the provider with a one lined input to enter his or her provider number. To receive that provider's health care services, the member hands his or her card to the provider who then slides the card through the card reader. The terminal pulls from the ChocAn Data Center and verifies the member's number on the card. The terminal also provides billing to ChocAn.

Service Code

A six-digit code used to represent a specific health care service.

Provider Directory

An alphabetically-ordered list used to look up the service code corresponding to the service provided. Can be sent as an email when requested by a provider.

Record

Written after a service code is selected. Contains current date and time, date service was provided, provider number, member number, service code, and optional comments.

Provider Form

A form the provider fills out for verification purposes after looking up the fee to be paid. Contains the current time and date, the date the service was provided, member name and number, service code, and the fee to be paid.

Member Report

An email attachment sent to members who received care that week. Sent at midnight on Friday or when requested by a manager. Contains member name, member number, and member's whole address. For each service provided, the date of the service, the provider name, and the service name are also included.

Provider Report

An email attachment sent to each provider who has billed ChocAn during the week. Contains the same information that is on the provider's form, in the order that the data were received, as well as a summary with the amount of consultations and the total fee for the week.

Summary Report

A list of every provider to be paid for that week, the number of consultations each had, and his or her total fee for the week. The total number of providers who provided services, the total number of consultations, and each provider's overall fee are printed as well.

Interactive Mode

A mode of software operation that allows members and providers to be added, removed, and updated in real time.

Project 2 Task 1 Summary

All interactions between terminal and data center and all changes and updates to the system and its directory will be computerized. The data for this project will be stored in the ChocAn data center, most likely on harddisk. Required hardware will be terminals for the provider, manager, and operator as well as a server in the facility. The ChocAn software will provide the EFT data to the Financial system and interact with communication software between the terminals and the server. Through the terminal systems and command line the users will interact with the ChocAn system.
